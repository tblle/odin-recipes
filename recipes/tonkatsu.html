Tonkatsu

Description

Tonkatsu is a quick and simple dish that originated from Japan. It is a crispy fried pork cutlet with curry sauce generously drizzled on top. It typically has thinly shredded cabbage to balance out the meal.

Ingredients

Fried Pork
  • 1 pork cutlet
  • 25g starch (potato or corn)
  • 50g panko
  • 1 egg
  • salt
  • black pepper
  • Enough corn oil to cover pork when deep frying
Curry Sauce
  • 3g curry roux
  • 2 cleaned carrots
  • 1 medium peeled potato
  • 1 vidalia onion
  • 15g neutral oil
  • 15g soy sauce
  • 250g vegetable/chicken/beef broth

Steps for Fried Pork

  1. Gently hammer pork cutlet until 2-3cm thick
  2. Pat dry pork cutlet with paper towel
  3. Sprinkle a pinch of salt on pork cutlet
  4. Beat egg and mix in a pinch of salt and black pepper. Absorb pork cutlet in egg mixture.
  5. Remove pork from egg mixture and cover entire pork with the starch. Repeat steps 4 and 5 but replace starch with panko.
  6. Heat up oil until 170c (or drop some leftover panko and see if it bubbles). Fry pork for 3 minutes or until internal temperature reaches 71c.

Steps for Curry Sauce

  1. Chop carrots, potato, and onion into 2cm cubes.
  2. Fry vegetables in oil until soften, then add roux and soy sauce.
  3. Slowly add broth into pot, while stirring so roux can be incorporated smoothly into broth.
Serve fried pork over shredded cabbage and drizzle as much curry sauce as you desire.
